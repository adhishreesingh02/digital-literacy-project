# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Fraud

In today’s digital world, UPI payments have become very common because they are quick and convenient. However, with this convenience, cases of UPI fraud have also increased, especially among students and people who are not fully aware of online scams.

A common situation is when a fraudster calls or messages a person pretending to be a bank official or customer care executive. They usually create panic by saying that there is a problem with the user’s account, such as it being blocked or needing urgent verification. Then, they send a link or a payment request and ask the user to approve it. Many people think they are receiving money, but in reality, they end up authorizing a payment.

In most cases, students and new users are targeted because they may not clearly understand how UPI requests work. The result can be a direct loss of money and sometimes even misuse of personal information.

This type of fraud can be avoided by staying alert. One should never share their UPI PIN or OTP with anyone and should always check requests carefully before approving them. Being aware and cautious is the best way to stay safe online.
